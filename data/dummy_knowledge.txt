Worst Cyclones in History

1. Great Bhola Cyclone (1970)
Location: East Pakistan (now Bangladesh)
Date: November 11, 1970
Details: Recognized as the deadliest tropical cyclone ever recorded. It struck East Pakistan with winds of up to 185 km/h (115 mph).
Impact: Estimated 300,000 to 500,000 fatalities, primarily due to a massive storm surge that flooded low-lying islands in the Ganges Delta.

2. Super Typhoon Tip (1979)
Location: Western Pacific (Guam, Japan)
Date: October 1979
Details: The most intense tropical cyclone on record by air pressure (870 hPa) and the largest by diameter (2,220 km).
Impact: While it weakened before hitting Japan, it still caused widespread flooding and 99 deaths.

3. Cyclone Nargis (2008)
Location: Myanmar
Date: May 2, 2008
Details: A Category 4 cyclone that caused catastrophic destruction in the Ayeyarwady Delta.
Impact: Over 138,000 people were killed or went missing. It caused $10 billion in damages, making it the costliest cyclone in the Indian Ocean basin.

4. Typhoon Haiyan (Yolanda) (2013)
Location: Philippines, Vietnam, China
Date: November 8, 2013
Details: One of the strongest tropical cyclones to make landfall, with sustained winds of 315 km/h (195 mph).
Impact: Devastated Tacloban City; over 6,300 deaths confirmed in the Philippines alone.

5. Hurricane Katrina (2005)
Location: United States (Gulf Coast)
Date: August 2005
Details: The costliest natural disaster in US history.
Impact: Caused over 1,800 deaths and $125 billion in damage, largely due to levee failures in New Orleans.
